18. The theory corner: Jianlin Su and the analytic threads (RoPE, attention scaling, optimizers)

Most of the knobs a 10T/200B GB200 run will actually touch — the RoPE base you schedule for 128K context, the attention temperature you bolt on for length extrapolation, the optimizer geometry that lets you reuse an AdamW learning rate under Muon, and the FP32 islands you preserve inside an otherwise-FP8 attention kernel — were given closed-form derivations on one Chinese-language blog before they shipped in any frontier paper. Jianlin Su (苏剑林), writing at kexue.fm / spaces.ac.cn ("Scientific Spaces"), is the single most under-cited primary source for the analytic justifications behind position encoding, attention scaling, and optimizer-geometry choices now standard at the frontier. This chapter walks the four threads of his corpus that map directly onto your build — RoPE base/length-generalization, the log-n entropy-invariance attention scale, the muP↔︎Muon spectral-geometry unification, and biased low-precision rounding in attention — and ties each analytic result to a specific knob, then connects them to the adjacent Western analytic voices (Keller Jordan, Greg Yang, the attention-entropy-collapse line) that frontier teams cite by name. A practical note before starting: kexue.fm is a client-rendered SPA behind Cloudflare that blocks scrapers, so the canonical text is most reliably reached via the Wayback Machine, verbatim Zhihu/CSDN reposts, or Tyler Romero's faithful English translations (Romero, Scientific Spaces translations). Treat the URLs below as kexue.fm/archives/N with the spaces.ac.cn/archives/N mirror as fallback.

18.1 RoPE as a β-ary code: base selection and length generalization

Su's "Transformer升级之路" ("Transformer Upgrade Path") series is the canonical RoPE corpus, and entry #2, 博采众长的旋转式位置编码 (archives/8265, 2021-03-23), is the original derivation of RoPE itself. But for a frontier long-context run the load-bearing entry is #10, RoPE是一种β进制编码 ("RoPE is a β-ary encoding," archives/9675), because it supplies the abstraction that makes every subsequent length-extrapolation trick legible. The observation: with rotary frequencies θ_i = b^(−2i/d), each RoPE dimension pair behaves like one digit of position written in base b — high-index dimensions are the low-frequency, slow-rotating "high-order digits," low-index dimensions are the fast-rotating "low-order digits." Once you see RoPE as a positional number system, the three length-extension strategies fall out as arithmetic on that representation:

Strategy Operation on the β-ary code Failure / cost
Direct extrapolation Emit position values larger than any seen in training High-order digits take unseen values → OOD, breaks
Position Interpolation (PI) Divide every position by scale factor k Compresses local structure (digits become fractional) → needs fine-tuning
NTK-aware scaled RoPE Rescale base b so high-freq digits extrapolate, low-freq digits interpolate Training-free; distributes the "extrapolation pressure" across digits

From this lens Su re-derives NTK-aware scaling as the slogan "high-frequency extrapolation, low-frequency interpolation" — the fast-rotating digits, which have already cycled many times within the training window, can safely extrapolate; the slow-rotating digits, which have not completed a cycle, must interpolate (archives/9675). This is exactly why a simple base rescale buys training-free context extension where naive PI does not, and #11, 将β进制位置进行到底 (archives/9706), pushes it to a mixed-radix generalization.

The entry that resolves a question every DeepSeek/Llama practitioner hits is #18, RoPE的底数选择原则 ("Principles of RoPE base selection," archives/10122, 2024-05-29). Su's result, reframing and extending the "Base of RoPE Bounds Context Length" analysis (the same critical-dimension framing as arXiv:2310.05209): the base b = θ is not a free hyperparameter — there is a lower bound on b set by the training length. Below that bound, the lowest-frequency dimensions never complete even a fraction of a rotation across the context, so the model literally cannot encode the long-range relative positions it is being asked to attend over; the attention becomes position-degenerate at long range. The practical consequence is unambiguous and is now standard practice: long-context runs must raise the θ-base commensurately with sequence length, and this is independent of the short-then-long fine-tune recipe. This is the analytic justification for the migration from b = 10⁴ at 2–4K context to b ≈ 10⁶ and beyond at 128K+ that you see across the frontier (Llama-3's 5×10⁵, DeepSeek's YaRN-extended bases, Qwen's long-context bases).

Recommendation for the 10T/200B target. Treat the RoPE base as a scheduled quantity, not a constant. If your pretraining native window is 8K and you extend to 128K (a 16× factor), #18's bound says the base must rise super-linearly relative to the naive 10⁴ — budget for b in the 10⁶–10⁷ range at the long-context stage, set at the start of the long stage rather than annealed, and verify the critical-dimension condition (that even your lowest-frequency rotary pair completes a meaningful fraction of a cycle at max position). Because your attention is MLA (Chapter 17), the RoPE lives only on the decoupled qk_rope_head_dim=64 slice; the base schedule applies to that slice, and the YaRN mscale correction (below) is absorbed into softmax_scale.

ReRoPE — the still-underused alternative. Entry #12, 无限外推的ReRoPE？ ("Infinitely-extrapolating ReRoPE?", archives/9708, 2023-08-07), with the HWFA follow-up #14 当HWFA遇见ReRoPE (archives/9731), proposes Rectified RoPE: pick a window w; inside the window use position step 1, outside use a reduced step 1/k (Leaky ReRoPE keeps a small nonzero slope continuing linearly outside, rather than a hard clamp). The relative-position matrix is rectified — ReLU-shaped, hence the name — so the maximum relative position the model ever sees never exceeds the training length, giving "approximately infinite" extrapolation with no fine-tuning at all. The reported PPL beats YaRN. The reason it did not displace YaRN is purely systems: ReRoPE needs two distinct attention patterns (inside-window with step 1, outside-window with step 1/k), i.e. effectively two attention matrices computed at inference, which is a non-starter for a latency-sensitive serving stack (archives/9708). For your build, ReRoPE is best understood as the conceptual parent of the chunked/windowed-plus-global attention schemes (Llama-4-style ChunkedCausalMask, the sliding-window branch of NSA) that you will likely run anyway — the tradeoff ReRoPE makes explicit (extra inference compute in exchange for training-free, unbounded extension) is exactly the one to weigh when you choose between a YaRN-style scalar correction and a structural windowing scheme. If you are already paying for a sparse/windowed long-context path, the ReRoPE rectification is nearly free to fold in; if you are running dense attention to the full context, YaRN is cheaper.

18.2 The log-n thread: attention scale from entropy invariance

The second thread is the one that quietly explains an entire family of frontier stability fixes. The seed is 从熵不变性看Attention的Scale操作 ("Attention's scale operation viewed from entropy invariance," archives/8823, 2021-12). Su's argument: the entropy of an attention distribution measures its "focus" — 0 when all mass is on one token, log n when uniform over n tokens. The standard 1/√d scaling was derived to keep the variance of the pre-softmax logits O(1) at initialization, but it says nothing about what happens to the distribution's focus as the sequence length n grows. Empirically, as n grows the attention dilutes: tokens that were sharply attended get washed out simply because there are more competitors. Imposing the principle that attention focus should be invariant to n — that adding far-away tokens should not blunt the model's attention to nearby ones — and solving for the scale that holds entropy constant yields:

Attention = softmax( (κ · log n / d) · QKᵀ ) · V

with κ an n-, d-independent constant. Su labels this Attention-E (entropy-invariant) versus the original Attention-O (1/√d), and crucially flags that the naive ACL-2022 variant softmax((log n/√d)·QKᵀ)·V suffers vanishing gradients at large n because the 1/√d does not absorb the dimension growth correctly — his /d form avoids it (archives/8823). What gives this thread unusual confidence is that Su re-derives the same log-n scale two more independent ways: from a fast entropy-invariance argument in 熵不变性Softmax的一个快速推导 (archives/9034), from the Johnson–Lindenstrauss lemma in 从JL引理看熵不变性Attention (archives/9588), and from gradient maximization in 从梯度最大化看Attention的Scale操作 (archives/9812). Three orthogonal derivations landing on log-n is strong evidence that the YaRN/QK-norm/logit-cap family of fixes is solving one underlying problem — uncontrolled attention-entropy dynamics as n grows — not three coincidentally-related ones.

The frontier connection is direct and verifiable. YaRN's attention temperature sets √(1/t) = 0.1·ln(s) + 1, where s is the context-extension factor (fitted across Llama 7B–65B) (YaRN, arXiv:2309.00071). That is a logarithmic scaling of the attention logits as a function of how far you are extrapolating — precisely Su's entropy-invariant log-n correction, re-instantiated as a length-extrapolation knob. In MLA codebases this lands as the mscale term 0.1·mscale·ln(rope_factor) + 1 folded into softmax_scale when max_position_embeddings > original — the YaRN temperature and the entropy-invariant scale are the same object.

The same family explains why two other now-standard stabilizers work:

  • QK-norm + learnable logit scale (ViT-22B, arXiv:2302.05442): LayerNorm on Q and K before the dot product caps the logit magnitude structurally, and ViT-22B adopted it specifically to stop divergence from uncontrolled attention-logit growth observed around 8B parameters — the empirical counterpart to bounding attention entropy from below.
  • Gemma-style logit soft-capping: tanh-capping the pre-softmax logits is, again, a control on attention entropy as n grows.
  • σReparam (Zhai et al., Apple, ICML 2023, arXiv:2303.06296): the Stabilizing Transformer Training by Preventing Attention Entropy Collapse paper shows directly that low attention entropy correlates with training divergence, and proves a theorem lower-bounding attention entropy by the spectral norm of W_qᵀW_k. Their fix — reparametrize every linear layer as (γ/‖W‖_spec)·W with a learned scalar γ — is the entropy-invariance principle pushed into the weight parametrization itself. This is the bridge to thread 18.3: the same spectral norm that bounds attention entropy is the norm Muon constrains.

Recommendation for the 10T/200B target. You want an n-dependent (log-n) attention temperature, applied one of two ways. (1) Train-time: bake the entropy-invariant κ·log n/d scale into the attention op so focus is invariant across your training sequence-length curriculum (useful if you ramp sequence length during pretraining). (2) Inference-time: keep 1/√d at train and apply the YaRN √(1/t) = 0.1·ln(s)+1 correction only when extrapolating. Given that MLA already absorbs mscale into softmax_scale, route (2) is the lower-risk default — but pair it with QK-norm or a logit soft-cap as the structural backstop, since under Muon (18.3) the W_qᵀW_k spectral norm is actively driven upward and the σReparam theorem says that is exactly what collapses attention entropy and triggers the logit blow-ups Kimi-K2 fought with QK-Clip. The log-n thread tells you these are not three independent patches; controlling attention entropy once, structurally, closes the whole family.

18.3 One geometry: muP, the spectral condition, and Muon

This is Su's most consequential recent thread for a 100k-GB200 run, because it unifies initialization, learning-rate transfer, and optimizer choice under a single object — the spectral norm of weight matrices and their updates — and tells you how to co-design all three for a trillion-scale config.

The Muon side. Muon优化器赏析：从向量到矩阵的本质跨越 ("Appreciating Muon: the essential leap from vector to matrix," archives/10592, 2024-12-10) is Su's reading of Keller Jordan's Muon. Muon maintains momentum M_t = βM_{t−1} + G_t and updates W_t = W_{t−1} − η_t[msign(M_t) + λW_{t−1}], where msign is the matrix-sign / orthogonal polar factor: via the SVD M = UΣVᵀ, msign(M) = U_{[:,:r]}V_{[:,:r]}ᵀ — it discards Σ entirely, setting every singular value to 1. Su's framing, which became the field's slogan, is that Muon is steepest descent under a spectral-norm constraint: where Adam equalizes update magnitude per element (a per-coordinate geometry), Muon equalizes it per singular direction (a spectral geometry), and is loss-scale invariant in a way Adam is not — scaling the loss by λ scales M by λ but msign zeros that out, so the update is unchanged (archives/10592). The companions are Muon续集：为什么我们选择尝试Muon？ (archives/10739) and the practitioner-facing Muon优化器指南：快速上手与关键细节 ("Muon Guide: quick start and key details," archives/11416, 2025-11-19). The guide flags the real production footgun: there are ≥4 slightly different Muon variants in circulation, each with a different LR convention, and a mis-set LR is the dominant failure mode. The key operational fact: the Moonlight-aligned variant matches Adam's update magnitude, so migrating from an existing AdamW recipe means you can reuse Adam's LR and weight decay directly — no re-sweep.

The muP side. Su's spectral-norm series ties Muon to maximal-update parametrization. 初探MuP introduces μP/μTransfer; 高阶muP：更简明但更高明的谱条件缩放 ("Higher-order muP: simpler but smarter spectral-condition scaling," archives/10795, 2025-03-24) reframes Yang–Simon–Bernstein's A Spectral Condition for Feature Learning (arXiv:2310.17813). The condition, stated elementarily: weight matrices and their updates should have spectral norm scaling like √(fan_out/fan_in), which yields O(1)-RMS activations and O(1)-RMS activation updates at every width — and from that single condition μP drops out without the full Tensor-Programs machinery. The MuP之上 ("Beyond MuP") series then closes the loop: #1 好模型的三个特征 ("three traits of a good model," archives/11340, 2025-10-21) and #4 坚守参数的稳定性 (archives/11729). Su's punchline is the unification most readers miss: Muon and muP start from opposite ends — "Momentum Orthogonalized by Newton-Schulz" (an optimizer) versus "Maximal Update Parametrization" (an init/LR scheme) — yet converge on the same spectral geometry. muP says keep the update's spectral norm at √(fan_out/fan_in); Muon's msign enforces a unit-spectral-norm update that you then rescale to that target. muP explains why you want the spectral-norm constraint that Muon mechanically imposes. This is also why the σReparam theorem (18.2) and Muon are the same story from a third angle: all three are statements about controlling weight spectral norms.

The frontier recipe you will copy. Moonlight (Moonshot/Kimi report) is the first public account of Muon trained past 1B — a 16B-total MoE with ~3B active on ~5.7T tokens — and it made Muon scale with two non-obvious fixes that fall straight out of the spectral framing:

  1. Add weight decay. Plain Muon lets weight spectral norms drift unbounded across a long run; decoupled λW decay holds them.
  2. Per-parameter update-RMS matching to AdamW via a scale ≈ 0.2·√(max(fan_in, fan_out)). The bare msign output has RMS ≈ 1/√max(fan_in, fan_out) — a shape-dependent magnitude — so this factor rescales every matrix's effective update RMS to a constant ≈ 0.2, which is AdamW's empirically-observed update RMS. That is what lets a single shared LR, reused verbatim from AdamW, serve matrices of every shape.

Plus a ZeRO-1-style distributed Muon so the Newton–Schulz iterations and momentum shard across the optimizer-state partition. The headline: ~2× sample efficiency / ~52% of AdamW's compute for matched loss (Moonlight). (As Chapter 10 argues, the durable advantage is large-batch robustness more than a clean 2×; budget the saving conservatively but adopt Muon for the batch-size resilience your 100k-GPU data-parallel width forces on you.)

Recommendation for the 10T/200B target. Run the Moonlight stack — Muon on 2D hidden weights, AdamW on embeddings/head/norms, λ=0.1 on both, update-RMS rescale to ≈0.2 (or V4's 0.18) so you inherit an AdamW LR — and use the spectral-condition framing operationally in two ways. First, zero-shot LR transfer via μP: tune the LR (and other HPs) on a 13M–40M-parameter proxy and transfer to the full 10T model, the way Greg Yang's μTransfer transferred from sub-40M proxies to 6.7B GPT-3 at ~7% of total tuning cost (Tensor Programs V, arXiv:2203.03466) — the spectral condition is what makes that transfer valid under Muon, not just Adam. Second, monitor per-layer update spectral norm against the √(fan_out/fan_in) target as a first-class training metric; deviation is your earliest warning that a layer is leaving the feature-learning regime, and per-layer it tells you which projections need more Newton–Schulz polishing (the final-layer projections are known to be the first to outrun a fixed-step iteration at scale).

18.4 Biased rounding in low-precision attention — the FP8/GB200 thread

For a run that lives or dies on FP8 throughput, the most important single post in Su's corpus is 低精度Attention可能存在有偏的舍入误差 ("Low-precision attention may have biased rounding errors," archives/11371). It goes beyond the now-well-known result that BF16 breaks RoPE's relative-position invariance at large absolute positions — the When Precision Meets Position result (arXiv:2411.13476), which shows the RoPE error grows with absolute position and is driven largely by the first token, and whose fix, AnchorAttention (pin token 0 to a shared anchor position ID), cuts training time >50%. Su identifies a subtler and nastier failure: a systematically biased — not merely noisy — rounding error in attention accumulation.

The mechanism: BF16 addition of two same-sign, same-exponent operands carries and truncates the mantissa, and round-to-nearest injects a small positive bias on each such addition. Normally this is cancelled by round-to-even (banker's rounding) at the exact half-way ties. But when you add a third, tiny operand — exactly the situation in long-sequence attention accumulation, where you are summing many product terms of widely varying magnitude — the third operand perturbs the value off the exact tie, the round-to-even tie-breaking rule no longer fires, and the positive bias is re-introduced systematically and accumulates over the sequence (archives/11371). Critically, this is bias, not variance: it does not average out over many tokens; it grows with sequence length, so long context and low precision interact adversarially in a directional way. This is a different and worse failure than the random rounding noise most FP8 ablations test for.

The systems consequences for GB200/Blackwell:

  • RoPE sin/cos must stay FP32. Both the When Precision Meets Position result and basic translational-invariance arguments demand it; never compute the rotary tables in BF16/FP8.
  • Attention-logit accumulation needs FP32 (or DeepSeek-style high-precision accumulation). torch.matmul is already "BF16 in, FP32 accumulate," which is why it mostly dodges this. But the Hopper/Blackwell FP8 tensor-core path accumulates in a reduced FP22/FP23 format, which is exactly the regime where Su's biased-rounding mechanism bites — this is what forced DeepSeek to write custom DeepGEMM kernels that promote accumulation off the tensor-core path into higher precision. On your FP8 attention kernels (FA4's CuTe-DSL path, or a DSA-style sparse path), verify the accumulator precision explicitly; do not assume the tensor-core native accumulate is safe for long-context attention.
  • AnchorAttention is cheap insurance for the first-token RoPE error and stacks cleanly with FP32 accumulation.

Recommendation for the 10T/200B target. Adopt a precision-island discipline in the attention path: FP8 for the QKᵀ and PV matmul inputs, FP32 for the rotary tables, the softmax/logit accumulation, the DSA indexer's weights_proj (keep it FP32 and unquantized regardless — it is load-bearing for sparse-selection stability), and any running-sum reductions over the sequence. Treat Su's biased-rounding result as a hard constraint on accumulator precision, not a tunable — the bias is directional and grows with your 128K–1M context targets, so it is precisely the regime where a "looks fine at 4K" FP8 ablation will mislead you. This is almost unknown outside Chinese ML circles and is the single highest-leverage item in this chapter for an FP8 team.

18.5 Adjacent analytic voices and the throughline

Su's blog supplies the closed-form intuition; the Western analytic line supplies the named techniques and the empirical scale-ups frontier teams cite directly. The map:

Analytic result (Su) Named technique / paper Frontier knob
β-ary RoPE; base lower-bound (#10, #18) YaRN, NTK-aware, critical-dimension (arXiv:2310.05209) RoPE-base schedule for 128K+ context
ReRoPE rectification (#12) Chunked/windowed+global attention Llama-4 chunked mask; NSA sliding window
log-n entropy invariance (#8823, #9588, #9812) YaRN temp √(1/t)=0.1·ln(s)+1; QK-norm (ViT-22B); σReparam (arXiv:2303.06296) n-dependent attention temperature; QK-norm; logit soft-cap
Muon = spectral-norm steepest descent (#10592) Muon (Jordan); modular norm (Large/Bernstein 2024) Muon on 2D hidden weights
Higher-order muP / spectral condition (#10795) Spectral Condition (arXiv:2310.17813); μTransfer (arXiv:2203.03466) μP LR transfer from <40M proxy; per-layer update-norm targets
Biased BF16 rounding in attention (#11371) When Precision Meets Position (arXiv:2411.13476); DeepGEMM FP32 accumulation islands in FP8 attention

Keller Jordan (Muon writeup, modded-nanogpt) is Su's complement on the systems side. The canonical Newton–Schulz quintic is φ(x) = ax + bx³ + cx⁵ with (a,b,c) = (3.4445, −4.7750, 2.0315), run for 5 iterations, driving singular values into [0.7, 1.3] — note this is deliberately not a convergent iteration to the identity; an ε ≈ 0.3 spectral tolerance is accepted because the coefficients are tuned to amplify small singular values fast, which is why 5 steps suffice where a convergent iteration would need 15–30. Muon set the NanoGPT speed record on 2024-10-15 (+35%) and held it through 12+ subsequent records; it applies only to hidden 2D layers (embeddings, heads, and scalars go to AdamW), and Jordan cites Bernstein et al.'s modular norm (Large et al. 2024) for why embedding dynamics differ — the same layer-wise split Su's spectral analysis predicts. Tyler Romero's living NanoGPT-speedrun worklog plus his Su translations are the single best bridge between the Chinese-language analytic corpus and the speedrun engineering it informs (Romero).

Greg Yang's Tensor Programs IV/V (arXiv:2203.03466) is the root of Su's "initial-condition" spectral analysis: μP/μTransfer's demonstration that you can tune on 13M–40M proxies and zero-shot transfer to BERT-large / 6.7B GPT-3 at ~7% of total pretraining tuning cost is the empirical license for the proxy-tuning workflow above. And the attention-entropy-collapse line — Zhai et al.'s σReparam (arXiv:2303.06296) with its theorem lower-bounding attention entropy by ‖W_qᵀW_k‖_spec, plus ViT-22B's independent adoption of QK-norm at ~8B scale (arXiv:2302.05442) — is the empirical counterpart to Su's entropy-invariance scale, and the reason these threads are not separable: the spectral norm that σReparam bounds attention entropy with is the same norm Muon constrains and μP scales.

One last thread worth knowing if you consider a hybrid attention layout (Chapter 17): Su's 线性注意力简史：从模仿、创新到反哺 ("A brief history of linear attention," archives/11033, 2025-06) is a mid-2025 synthesis of the linear-attention lineage into the modern gated/delta variants (Gated DeltaNet, KDA) now reappearing in production hybrid stacks — written by someone who built several of the early variants (the 2020 探索 post archives/7546, the infinite-dimensional view archives/8601, FLASH archives/8934), and including the expressivity-limits argument 注意力机制真的可以"集中注意力"吗 (archives/9889) that explains why you keep a 1-in-3 to 1-in-7 ratio of full/MLA layers rather than going fully linear.

The throughline. Su's corpus is the closed-form layer underneath a set of knobs frontier teams ship as engineering: the RoPE-base schedule (β-ary, #18), the log-n attention temperature (entropy invariance, YaRN), the QK-norm/logit-cap family (entropy collapse, σReparam), the Muon+μP recipe (one spectral geometry from two directions), and the FP32-accumulation islands in FP8 attention (biased rounding). For a 10T/200B/100k-GB200 build the practical payoff of reading him is that these stop looking like five independent patches and start looking like four analytic principles — positional number systems, entropy invariance, spectral-norm geometry, and directional low-precision bias — each of which tells you not just what knob to set but why, and therefore how it will behave when you push it to a scale no published run has reached.
