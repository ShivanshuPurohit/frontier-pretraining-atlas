FMThe State of Frontier Pretraining, July 2026

The question the book answers, how to read it, three reading paths, and the executive summary.

Part IThe Evidence Base

1Reading the Closed Frontier: Sources, Provenance, Method

Sets the sourcing discipline and demonstrates tier-laundering with a dated worked example; maps the 2026 source landscape — closed-lab disclosure, insider journalism, podcasts as primary sources, the conference/benchmark tier — and gives Epoch AI's estimation methodology and structural staleness full treatment; closes with a lab-by-lab triangulation table and a three-tier taxonomy of what is knowable, estimable, and genuinely dark.

2What the Closed Labs Let Slip

Every load-bearing pretraining disclosure from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta, xAI, and Microsoft during 2024-2026, lab by lab, including the Behemoth failure post-mortem and what it implies for a 10T/200B run's routing, data-pipeline, and evals discipline.

3The Open Frontier: China's Labs

Every major Chinese frontier lab except DeepSeek — Moonshot, Alibaba, Zhipu, MiniMax, StepFun, ByteDance, Ant Group, Tencent, Baidu — normalized into a master comparison table, reading the aggregate for converged 2026 practice against a genuine unresolved fork.

4DeepSeek: The Reference Open Stack

The V3/V3.2/V4 lineage as the reference implementation: DualPipe, the FP8 blockwise recipe, MLA as a memory-bandwidth optimization, aux-loss-free balancing, the full hyperparameter and cost tables, Fire-Flyer 2, V4's unresolved deltas, and a repo tour of DeepGEMM/EPLB/DeepEP/FlashMLA/3FS.

Part IIThe Model

5Anatomy of a 2026 Frontier Model

The decoder ring for modern model cards: the invariant transformer skeleton and every 2026 substitution inside it — residual-stream re-plumbing (Hyper-Connections, mHC, Attention Residuals), the MoE block from first principles through aux-loss-free balancing, Quantile Balancing, and V4's hash-routed early layers, MTP, and full dissections of DeepSeek-V4-Pro, Kimi K3, and Nemotron-3 Ultra.

6The Attention Zoo: Softmax, Selection, and the Delta Rule

KV-cache arithmetic as the enemy, then the three families that fight it: latent compression (MLA, taught via the absorption trick and Su Jianlin's decomposition), trainable sparse selection (NSA → MoBA → DSA → V4's CSA/HCA, MiniMax's MSA), and linear attention actually explained — associative memory, decay gates, the delta rule as test-time learning, Gated DeltaNet, KDA — plus why every frontier deployment hybridizes.

7The MoE Design Space and a 10T/200B Strawman

Fixes the architecture the later chapters assume: activation ratio, attention mechanism, routing, granularity, multi-token prediction, the Nemotron-3 hybrid Mamba-MoE alternative branch, and a fully justified strawman spec table.

8Scaling Laws as Practiced on Proprietary Data

Reconstructs the coupled ladder of power laws frontier labs actually fit — learning rate, batch size, sparsity, precision, repetition — muP/muTransfer's real reach, critical-batch-size practice, and a costed recipe for commissioning a laws program ahead of a 10T/200B run.

9Optimizers and Stability at 10T Parameters

Builds the evidence base for a one-shot optimizer bet under genuine extrapolation: Muon's mechanics and validation ceiling, distributed Newton-Schulz engineering, adversarial re-benchmarks, a stability toolkit, and a four-stage de-risking ladder.

10Precision: FP8 to MXFP8 to NVFP4

Traces low-precision pretraining from DeepSeek's blockwise FP8 through NVIDIA's recipe zoo into MXFP8's Blackwell-native validation and NVFP4's early existence proof; resolves the GB200 NVL72 FLOPS conflation; closes with a bet-the-run-versus-pilot-only verdict.

11Multimodal-Native Pretraining as the 2026 Default

Argues multimodal-native pretraining is now the field default; maps three coexisting MoE-multimodal integration patterns, tokenizer and curriculum questions, and closes with a provisioning checklist for a primarily-text host.

Part IIIThe Data

12The Data Supply Chain: Crawlers, Licensing, and the Stock

Reconciles what a 10T-parameter host needs against what demonstrably exists: crawler telemetry, Common Crawl's enclosure, licensing-deal economics, book-acquisition litigation exposure, and the stock-math reconciliation against Epoch's ~300T-token estimate.

13Curation SOTA and What Lies Beyond Essential-Web

Dissects Essential-Web v1.0 as the reference curation architecture, maps the full open-dataset frontier, works technique-by-technique through measured deltas, and closes with a 2026 reference data-factory blueprint.

14Midtraining, Mixtures, and the Endgame Curriculum

Assembles the field's only token-level ground truth (Ai2's Dolmino) alongside Phi-4, SmolLM3, and Nemotron-3's phase ladders; quantifies long-context extension's regression cost; commits to a costed, staged 28T-token curriculum.

15The Input Pipeline: Dataloading at 100k GPUs

Shows raw dataloading is never the throughput bottleneck at 100,000 GPUs; the real risks are orchestration — cold-start stalls, multi-source workload imbalance, and a confirmed gap in per-example metadata-conditional sampling — scored across every major loader.

Part IVThe Machine

16GB200 NVL72 Ground Truth and the Alternative Silicon Question

Establishes the corrected hardware ground truth beneath every FLOPs-per-dollar claim that follows; walks the H100-vs-GB200 benchmark record, reliability findings, and per-platform verdicts on Trainium, TPU, Ascend, and AMD.

17Kernels for Blackwell: FlashAttention-4, Grouped GEMM, and the Fusion Inventory

Inventories the Blackwell kernel layer the run depends on: tcgen05's programming model, FlashAttention-4 in depth, the small-M MoE GEMM shape-efficiency problem, and an evidence-backed gap list of what must be built or funded.

18The Parallelism Math for 10T/200B on 100k GB200

Derives the parallelism math from first principles using the JAX Scaling Book's roofline algebra; works the MoE-specific formulas and shows why pure DP/FSDP is comms-bound while ZeRO-1-plus-EP escapes the same trap; closes with three fully-worked candidate layouts.

19Frameworks: Megatron, TorchTitan, MaxText, and the Field

Evaluates the frameworks a 10T/200B run would build on at the code and issue-tracker level; closes with an explicit fork-vs-build recommendation for Megatron-Core and a prioritized modification list.

20Networking at 100k: NVL72 Domains, Fabrics, MRC, and Collectives

Maps the network fabric from the NVL72 scale-up domain outward: the reference-architecture gap above ~10,000 GPUs, MRC against UEC/Falcon/EFA, NCCL's evolution, and multi-datacenter training's physical limits.

Part VThe Run

21Fault Tolerance, Checkpointing, and Goodput

Establishes the failure-rate ground truth for a 100,000-GB200 fleet, GB200's distinct reliability profile, silent-data-corruption mitigation, checkpoint math for 140TB of state, and a six-layer reliability spec with an 85-93% goodput target.

22Operations: War Stories, Debugging Doctrine, and Running the Run

Reads the operational layer as its own genre: a five-class incident taxonomy, bare-metal war stories, Stas Bekman's debugging doctrine, and a concrete war-room and operational-readiness checklist for a six-month run.

23Benchmarks and the Money Math

Builds the only defensible cost model for the run, working from audited or vendor-published ground truth: MLPerf's evolution, MFU derived step-by-step from NVIDIA's own tables, DeepSeek-V3's MFU reconstructed and cross-validated, and the full money table.

Part VIThe Ledger

24The Gap List: What to Fix for 10T/200B on 100k GB200

The capstone: what a team must build, in what order, organized as seven systems, each a gap table with why-it-binds, evidence, build-vs-adopt call, effort class, and lead time — closing with a single-page master checklist by lead time.

25The Source Atlas: Annotated Bibliography and Surprise Index

The index and honesty ledger: all research notes indexed, a tiered annotated bibliography across fifteen clusters, a 36-item Surprise Index, and a "What We Could Not Verify" section stating the record's genuine gaps plainly.
