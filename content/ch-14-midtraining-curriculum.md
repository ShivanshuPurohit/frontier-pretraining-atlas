# Chapter 14 — Midtraining, Mixtures, and the Endgame Curriculum

Pretraining used to end where post-training began, with nothing in between but a learning-rate decay nobody wrote a paper about. By late 2025 that gap had acquired a name, a taxonomy, two competing surveys, and — from Ai2 in particular — token-level accounting precise enough to reproduce. This chapter works through what is actually known about midtraining, long-context extension, mixture science, and the reinforcement-pretraining fringe, then commits to a staged curriculum for the 10T-total/200B-active run.

## The Pipeline Gets a Third Leg: Definitions and Taxonomy

Two arXiv surveys appeared within three weeks of each other in October 2025 and converged on the same three-axis description of midtraining: a shift in **data distribution** toward curated, high-quality corpora; an interaction with **learning-rate scheduling** (decay timing and shape); and **long-context extension** ([Mid-Training of Large Language Models: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.06826) and [A Survey on LLM Mid-training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23081), both Oct 2025). Both credit the term itself to Phi and OpenAI usage before Ai2 formalized it in OLMo 2's own footnote ("Abdin et al. 2024a and OpenAI 2024" — [OLMo 2 Furious](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00656), §2.3 fn.4). A more mechanistic March 2026 follow-up, [PRISM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17074), asks *why* midtraining data doesn't erase pretraining capability — a retention question rather than a mixture question — and [Midtraining Bridges Pretraining and Posttraining Distributions](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.14865) (Oct 2025) frames the stage as closing the KL gap between raw web text and chat-formatted instructions, not merely as a quality upsample.

The practical upshot for a 10T/200B host: midtraining is no longer an ad hoc "anneal at the end" gesture. It is a distinct, budgeted, measured stage with its own data pool, its own LR trajectory, and — per the surveys — its own open failure modes (instruction-data placement chief among them, discussed below).

## The Gold-Standard Disclosures: OLMo 2 and OLMo 3's Dolmino Mixes

No frontier-adjacent lab publishes midtraining mixture data at the resolution Ai2 does. [OLMo 2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00656) (v3 Oct 2025, COLM 2025) splits training into **Stage 1 pretraining** (90-95% of FLOPs, cosine decay to 10% of peak LR, on a 3.90T-token mix: DCLM-Baseline 3.71T, StarCoder 83.0B, peS2o 58.6B, arXiv 20.8B, OpenWebMath 12.2B, Algebraic Stack 11.8B, Wikipedia 3.7B) and **Stage 2 midtraining** (5-10% of FLOPs, linear decay to zero, on Dolmino Mix 1124). The Dolmino source pool totals 843B tokens: an 832.6B-token high-quality subset (FastText-top-7%-filtered DCLM at 752B, decontaminated FLAN 17.0B, peS2o 58.6B, Wikipedia/Wikibooks 3.7B, curated StackExchange 1.26B) and a 10.7B-token math mix (TuluMath synthetic, Dolmino SynthMath, TinyGSM-MIND, MathCoder2 Synth Books, Metamath, CodeSearchNet, GSM8K train). From this pool they sample three fixed-size anneal mixes — 50B (7B model), 100B (13B), 300B (32B) — holding filtered-DCLM at a roughly constant **~50% share** across all three by explicit design (Table 13).

[OLMo 3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13961) (Dec 2025) is the richer disclosure. Its Stage 2 midtrain mix is 100B tokens sampled from a 2.19T-token pool, with every category costed to the tenth of a percent (Table 5):

| Category | Source | Tokens (of 100B) | % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Math (synth) | Dolmino Math + CraneMath + MegaMatt + TinyMATH | ~19.2B | ~19.2% |
| Code | StackEdu (FIM) + CraneCode | 20.0B | 20.0% |
| QA (synth) | Reddit-to-Flashcards, Nemotron Synth QA, Wiki-to-RCQA | 13.9B | 13.9% |
| Thinking (synth, 9 sources) | QWQ/Gemini/Llama-Nemotron/OpenThoughts2 + reasoning traces | ~9.0B | ~9.0% |
| Instruction (synth) | Dolmino-1 Flan + Tulu3 SFT | 6.1B | 6.1% |
| PDFs | olmOCR science PDFs | 4.99B | 5.0% |
| Web | STEM-heavy crawl + Common Crawl HQ | 27.4B | 27.5% |

Methodology matters as much as the numbers. OLMo 3 runs a two-tier "distributed exploration → centralized assessment" loop — cheap **microanneals** (5B candidate + 5B web, 10B total) feeding periodic **integration tests** (full 100B-token anneals, followed by quick SFT to confirm gains survive post-training). Five integration rounds moved OlmoBaseEval from 49.7 to 50.7 to a final 53.1 (Table 6) — a documented, monotonic mixture-quality trajectory, the kind of evidence trail almost no other lab discloses. A domain-tradeoff ablation quantifies the cost of upweighting math/code: a math-code-thinking-skewed mix scores Math 60.8/Code 35.6 against the balanced Round-5 mix's Math 57.1/Code 27.7, but GenQA drops 73.1→65.9 — roughly 7 points of general-QA capability for 4-8 points of math/code (Table 7).

## Cross-Lab Midtraining Practice: Phi-4, SmolLM3, and the Nemotron Phase Ladders

Outside Ai2, disclosures are coarser but still numerically anchored. [SmolLM3](https://huggingface.co/blog/smollm3) (Jul 2025) runs an 11.2T-token, three-stage curriculum with exact absolute-token boundaries: Stage 1 (0→8T, 85% web/12% code/3% math), Stage 2 (8T→10T, 75%/15%/10%), Stage 3 decay (10T→11.1T, 63%/24%/13%, adding OpenMathReasoning). [Phi-4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.08905) (Dec 2024) discloses a midtrain mix of **30% newly-curated long-context data / 70% recall (replay) tokens**, RoPE base raised 10K→250K, and peak LR cut **10x** relative to pretraining.

NVIDIA's Nemotron family gives the cleanest cross-generation pattern. [Nemotron Nano 2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.14444) (Aug-Sep 2025) transitions Phase 1→2 at **60%** of tokens and Phase 2→3 at **90%**. [Nemotron 3 Nano](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.20848) (Dec 2025) runs a two-phase 25T-token curriculum with the switch at **94%** (Phase 1: 23.5T/94% diversity-focused, Phase 2: 1.5T/6% quality-focused). [Nemotron 3 Super](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12374) (Apr 2026) — also 25T tokens — switches at **80%** (Phase 1: 20T/80%, Phase 2: 5T/20%), and Nemotron 3 Ultra switched at **75%** — 15T + 5T = **20T total**, a 25% quality share. But the Ultra paper discloses that the run's horizon was *cut from a planned 25T to 20T* after two mid-run divergences forced a rollback and early decay, which confounds the apparent gradient: at the planned 25T horizon, Ultra's 5T quality phase would have been 20% — flat with Super. The honest pattern is Nano 6% → Super/Ultra 20%: **bigger models in this family get proportionally more midtraining up to a point, and the oft-quoted 6→20→25 "gradient" is partly the arithmetic of a shortened run** — a caveat that matters when extrapolating a share to a 200B-active target (§ below on the mixture strawman). The same Nemotron Nano 2 report also gives a clean instruction-in-pretrain ablation: replacing 5% of a 100B continued-pretrain slice with synthetic "Fundamental Reasoning" SFT-style data lifted MMLU-Pro 44.24→56.36 (+12.1 pts) and Math 37.92→39.70 (+1.8 pts) (Table 3).

| Model | Total tokens | Quality/midtrain phase | Share | Transition point |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OLMo 2 7B/13B/32B | 3.90T + (50/100/300B) | 50B/100B/300B anneal | 1.3-7.7% | end of Stage 1 |
| OLMo 3 7B/32B | ~5.9T + 100B + 50-100B LC | 100B midtrain | 1.7% | end of Stage 1 |
| SmolLM3\* | 11.2T (8T/2T/1.1T) | Stage 2+3 (2T+1.1T) | ~28% | 8T (71%) |
| Nemotron Nano 2 | — | Phase 2+3 | (P1→2 at 60%, P2→3 at 90%) | 60% / 90% |
| Nemotron 3 Nano | 25T | 1.5T | 6% | 94% |
| Nemotron 3 Super | 25T | 5T | 20% | 80% |
| Nemotron 3 Ultra | 20T (planned 25T, cut after divergences) | 5T | 25% (20% at planned horizon) | 75% |

Sources as cited above. \*The SmolLM3 "~28% / 8T (71%)" figures are our own arithmetic on the HuggingFace blog's disclosed absolute-token stage boundaries (8T/2T/1.1T of 11.2T total), not a percentage stated verbatim in the source.

## Decay-Phase Upweighting, Quantified

The purest test of midtraining's leverage is OLMo 2's own ablation: **LR-decay-to-zero alone, no new data**, on a 50B-token anneal from a 4T-token checkpoint, moves MMLU (completion-format) by **+20 points** and OLMES by +4.4 ([OLMo 2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00656), Table 11). OLMo 3's Stage-1→Stage-2 jump on its Math aggregate is even sharper: **23.5→59.8 (+36.3 points) from 100B midtrain tokens — 1.7% of the total token budget** ([OLMo 3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13961), Table 13). The per-token leverage of the decay phase is, on these two numbers, an order of magnitude higher than pretraining's marginal token. One scheduler-level development belongs alongside these anneal results: Nemotron 3 Super trains with a **Warmup-Stable-Merge (WSM)** schedule that replaces the LR-decay phase with checkpoint merging entirely — the paper credits the stable-phase-plus-merge design with recovering what decay would have delivered while freeing roughly **16% of total training FLOPs** otherwise spent in the decay tail, and reports 1-2% average benchmark gains that persist through post-training (arXiv:2604.12374). For a curriculum built around "the decay phase is where the leverage lives," a production-validated schedule that *eliminates* the decay phase and pays for a quality-phase extension with the savings is a live design alternative, not a footnote.

The mechanism behind this leverage is OLMo's **microannealing** methodology: mix a candidate source 50/50 with generic web data, anneal on ~10B tokens with a proper LR decay, score on a 200-example GSM8K-derived dev set ("GSM*") plus MMLU. OLMo 2 ran 19 microanneals totaling 130B tokens — less than three full 50B anneal runs — to derive its final math mix (§4.4.2). Concrete findings from that sweep are directly reusable: domain-specific math helps even at a 10% ratio (65/35 web/math → GSM* 63.5; 90/10 → 61.0, both far above the 28.5 pre-anneal baseline); duplicating a small math dataset up to 4x still helps, with diminishing but positive returns (1x→63.5, 2x→66.0, 4x→65.0); and **rewriting code-formatted synthetic math into natural language is the single largest lever found** — raw TinyGSM in code-answer form actually *hurts* (GSM*=25.0, below baseline), while the MIND-rewritten natural-language version scores 65.5, and doubling that rewritten data reaches 70.0 (Table 12).

## Mixing Science: RegMix, Mixing Laws, CLIMB, and the $100M Question

The mixture-optimization literature is mature at small scale and essentially silent at frontier scale. [DoReMi](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.10429) (2023) — Group-DRO minimax reweighting via a 280M proxy for an 8B target — is 2026's standard *baseline*, not what any disclosed lab actually runs in production; every disclosed frontier lab (OLMo, Nemotron, SmolLM3, Qwen, Hunyuan) uses ablation/microanneal-style empirical search instead. [RegMix](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.01492) (ICLR 2025 Spotlight) trains 512 models at 1M params/1B tokens each, fits a LightGBM regression from mixture-proportion to loss, and applies the predicted-optimal mixture at a 1B/25B-token target — a proxy-search compute budget trivial next to the target run. Its clearest documented production lineage is Tencent's Hunyuan-MT translation line, which credits a RegMix-style search; the broader claim that it drives Hunyuan's flagship pretraining mixtures is not directly sourced (search-result-level sourcing, not checked against a primary document). [Data Mixing Laws](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.16952) (ICLR 2025) composes separately-fit scaling-in-steps, scaling-in-model-size, and mixing-law-in-proportions curves to predict loss on an unseen mixture, validated on a 1B/100B-token RedPajama target. [Nemotron-CLIMB](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.13161) (Apr 2025) embeds and clusters an *unlabeled* corpus into 20 semantic clusters — solving the "Common Crawl has no domain labels" problem RegMix and DoReMi both assume away — and reports a 1B/400B-token CLIMB-optimized model beating Llama-3.2-1B by 2.0% average, with domain-targeted optimization yielding a 5% within-domain improvement; the paper discloses **no scale-transfer evidence beyond 1B params**. [Aioli](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.05735) (ICLR 2025) is the one genuinely different idea in this cluster — an *online* method that fits mixing-law parameters continuously from the current run's own loss history rather than fixing a mixture upfront, beating stratified sampling by 0.27 test-perplexity points on average and by up to 12.0 points in the realistic setting where a proxy-learned mixture needs mid-run correction. That "matters most when the proxy mixture doesn't transfer cleanly" framing is precisely the risk profile of a 10T/200B run.

The most rigorous transfer evidence available — [Explaining Data Mixing Scaling Laws](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08167) (Jun 2026) and Apple's [Scaling Laws for Optimal Data Mixtures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.09404) (Jul 2025) — validates extrapolation from a 200M/8B-token proxy to a 700M/16B target, and separately from 122M/10B to 1B/30B: roughly 5-8x parameter scale-up, 2-3x token scale-up. No published paper we found extrapolates a mixture law across more than one order of magnitude in parameters. This is the honest answer to the $100M question: **there is no published evidence that a 1B-optimal mixture holds at 200B-active-parameter scale.** The closest available signal is negative-by-implication — the Nemotron 3 family's quality-phase token *fraction* changing with model size (6%→20%, with Ultra's nominal 25% confounded by its shortened horizon — above) is inconsistent with a scale-invariant optimal mixture, and is the strongest circumstantial argument that mixtures do not trivially transfer. Ch7 covers the analogous problem for architecture (granularity, activation ratio) and Ch8 covers the general scaling-law transfer literature in more depth; the mixture-specific ceiling documented here is narrower and, if anything, less resolved.

## Instruction Data in Pretraining and the Special-Token Landmine

Every disclosed, cleanly-ablated result finds instruction/chat-style data in pretraining positive. [Instruction Pre-Training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.14491) (Jun 2024) converts 1/5 of raw documents (40M docs) into ~200M synthetic instruction-response pairs (~10B tokens) and mixes them back with unchanged raw text. Nemotron Nano 2's 5%-substitution ablation (above) is unambiguously positive. OLMo 3's Table 10 ablation — full mix with thinking-traces and instruction data versus without, tokens held constant — improves every base-eval metric (Avg +1.9, Math +5.6, Code +1.1, FIM +2.7), with the paper's own framing: "this benefit begins even before post-training" ([OLMo 3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13961), §3.5.4). The October 2025 mid-training survey nonetheless calls the field's overall verdict "unresolved" ([arXiv:2510.06826](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.06826)) — and OLMo 3 supplies the likely reason.

They tested including chat special tokens (`<|im_start|>`, `<|im_end|>`) in midtrain instruction data. The model learns to emit these tokens at inference despite never being post-trained on them, which **collapses GSM8K from 49.43 to 0** and CruxEval from 32.89 to 18.91 — not because reasoning degraded, but because answer-parsing breaks on the unexpected tokens. A control using ordinary chat-template text without special tokens only drops GSM8K to 46.02, confirming the failure is vocabulary-artifact-specific, not chat-formatting-specific (§3.5.4). Their fix: strip special tokens from midtrain data entirely, use newline-based formatting, defer special tokens to SFT. Read against this finding, "instruction data in pretraining helps" is fairly robust; "chat-formatted-with-special-tokens data in pretraining" is a specific, avoidable footgun that the survey language conflates into a single unresolved question.

## Contamination Policy Reality

OLMo 3's open-sourced decontamination tool, `decon` (github.com/allenai/decon, Dec 2025), runs two phases: a sparse-stride n-gram detection pass for speed, then a dense cluster-expansion pass on hits to compute a contamination score without paying full-density-matching cost everywhere. They decontaminate **all splits** — train, val, and test — of every eval benchmark, because some benchmarks (FLAN among them) are literally built from benchmark templates and leak via train splits, not just test splits. Figure 12 of the report shows per-benchmark contamination counts and performance deltas: most are small (0 to ±2 points), but DROP, Minerva, and SQuAD show substantial score inflation when contaminated data isn't removed.

The counter-intuitive finding worth carrying forward: even where `decon` detected *complete* GSM8K leakage in some sources, removing it did not improve performance — attributed, per a public discussion of a similar Marin-32B finding, to the leaked text being in a different format than the eval harness expects, so the model doesn't actually recognize the leaked example as the benchmark question. Decontamination and eval-format-matching are more entangled than the "just decontaminate harder" instinct assumes; treat leakage removal as a hygiene and credibility requirement independent of its measured score impact, not a metric-optimization lever.

## Long-Context Extension: Recipes, Ratios, and Their Context-Parallel Cost

OLMo 3 §3.6 compiles the single best cross-lab long-context token-budget comparison available:

| Budget | Models |
|---|---|
| <20B tokens | Apple AFM, NVIDIA Nemotron Nano 2 (64K/128K targets) |
| ~20-100B tokens | SmolLM3 (100B), GLM-4.5 (100B) |
| 100-250B tokens | DeepSeek-V3 (123B), Apertus (225B) |
| 400B+ tokens | Kimi K2 (400B), Llama 3.1 (800B), DeepSeek-V3.1 (840B) |

That is a **50-100x** spread across disclosed practice, with no consensus on pipeline placement either: Llama 3.1 runs LC extension *before* midtraining, Qwen 2.5/3 and OLMo run it *after* midtraining and before SFT, GLM-4.5 runs it *after* SFT.

OLMo 3's own Stage 3 recipe is the most granular public description available. Source pool: the **Dolma 3 Longmino Pool**, 639B tokens of length-bucketed olmOCR-processed scientific PDFs, bucketed from 8K-16K (144B, 22.5%) up through 1M+ (26.9B, 4.21%). Final mix: **34% long-context / 66% short-context** (short-context = the Stage-2 midtrain mix), giving 50B tokens for the 7B model and 100B for the 32B model — larger model, proportionally more LC tokens, the same qualitative pattern as the Nemotron phase-size finding above (§3.6.3). The ratio itself is directly quantified at 10B-token scale: a 66% LC / 34% short-context mix costs 2.5 points of OlmoBaseEval versus equal-token short-only training; a 34% LC / 66% short mix costs only 0.8 points. That is the single most reusable exact number in this whole literature — going majority-long-context costs roughly 3x more short-context capability than staying majority-short.

Technique stack (§3.6.4): **YaRN applied only to full-attention layers, not sliding-window layers** (ablated directly against full-model YaRN and against 8M-theta variants, and winning); **best-fit document packing** rather than naive concatenate-then-split, which under-fills sequences relative to the true document-length distribution and alone gave a "substantial" RULER improvement; **intra-document attention masking**; and **8-way context parallelism**, all-gather-based rather than ring-based, chosen specifically because it tolerates the irregular attention masks that sliding-window plus intra-document masking produce. Final model souping for this stage used a cheaper substitute than multi-seed reruns: averaging the last three checkpoints of the single LC run (steps 10000/11000/11921).

Naturally-long documents beat artificially-concatenated ones for downstream long-context performance — confirmed independently a year apart by [Phi-4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.08905) (Dec 2024) and OLMo 3, and consistent with OLMo 3's best-fit-packing finding. [MiniMax-M1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13585) (Jun 2025) — a hybrid Lightning-Attention/softmax architecture — stages context growth **32K→1M gradually**, explicitly to avoid the gradient explosions observed when scaling context aggressively in a hybrid-attention setting; at 100K generation length it uses only 25% of DeepSeek-R1's FLOPs, a reminder that architecture choice changes the cost calculus of the LC stage independent of the mixing question (Ch7 owns the attention-architecture tradeoff itself).

The context-parallelism cost deserves stating explicitly rather than deferring entirely to Ch18: OLMo 3 needed 8-way CP for a 65K target. A 512K-1M target — the plausible ambition for a 10T/200B run given Kimi K2/DeepSeek-V3.1/Llama-3.1-class precedent — would scale roughly linearly with target length, implying 64-128-way CP as an *upper bound*; the directly disclosed production values are lower — Nemotron 3 Super ran CP64 and the larger Ultra CP32 at a real 1M-token target — so linear extrapolation from OLMo's 8-way overshoots what disclosed practice actually needed. That CP degree will materially depress MFU and tokens/s/GPU during Stage 3 specifically; budget a distinct, lower MFU target for the LC stage in the compute plan rather than assuming Stage-1 MFU carries through (Ch18 and Ch23 quantify the parallelism math and the resulting cost model).

## Souping and Branch-Train-Merge at Frontier Scale

Ai2's practice is the clearest disclosed example of anneal-phase souping: OLMo 2 7B trains **three separate 50B anneal runs** from the same Stage-1 checkpoint with different data shuffles, then weight-averages them; 13B/32B train **three 100B runs plus one 300B run** and average all four. Table 14's direct ablation shows souping "consistently equals or outperforms" the best single checkpoint on aggregate metrics — but per-metric variance is real: one mix's GSM8k score *dropped* 60.5→43.0 upon souping even as every other metric on that same mix improved ([OLMo 2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00656), Table 14). Souping is a net-positive, not a metric-monotonic, operation — a caveat worth carrying anywhere it gets presented as strictly dominant.

Two other souping-adjacent techniques belong in a different bucket and are worth distinguishing from Ai2's approach explicitly, since secondary coverage often conflates them. **Gemma 2's WARP** ([arXiv:2406.16768](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.16768), 2024) — EMA during RL fine-tuning, SLERP across RL-tuned policies, then linear interpolation back toward init — is a *post-training* RLHF merge technique, not a pretraining anneal-souping technique, despite the shared "souping" vocabulary. **Llama 4 Maverick's codistillation** from the never-fully-released Behemoth teacher (ai.meta.com/blog/llama-4-multimodal-intelligence, Apr 2025) is architecturally closer to Gemma 3's teacher-distillation pretraining (256 sampled logits per token, cross-entropy against a softened teacher distribution across all four Gemma 3 sizes — [arXiv:2503.19786](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19786), Mar 2025) than to weight-averaging; no distillation-loss-weight schedule was disclosed. Net read: souping/merging is dual-track in current frontier practice — anneal-souping (Ai2) squeezes variance out of the highest-leverage, lowest-token-count tail stage, while RLHF-policy-souping (Gemma's WARP lineage) combines reward-optimized policies in post-training. Nobody discloses souping across an entire pretraining run, only the tail stages — consistent with model-soup theory that linear mode connectivity holds best for checkpoints sharing most of their training trajectory.

A separate, distinct use of checkpoint-averaging is worth flagging for the measurement plan below: OLMo 3 cannot afford to fully anneal-to-zero at regular intervals for its 32B model, so it instead averages **four checkpoints, 1,000 steps apart**, as a cheap proxy for a clean mid-run evaluation signal ([OLMo 3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13961), §3.4, citing Li et al. 2025). This is an eval-noise-reduction trick, distinct from final-stage souping, and reusable throughout Stages 1-3.

## Reinforcement Pretraining: RPT, RLP, and the MAI-1 Counter-Example

Reinforcement Pre-training ([RPT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.08007), Jun 2025) reframes next-token prediction as an RL problem: the model receives a binary/sparse verifiable reward for correctly predicting the ground-truth next token via prefix-matching, with compute-vs-accuracy scaling claimed to improve consistently with more RPT compute. Its 2026 successor, [RLP](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01265) (Sep 2025, rev. Mar 2026, ICLR 2026, NVIDIA authorship), is explicitly critical of RPT's design: RPT's sparse binary reward "often relies on proxy-model filtering of 'easy' tokens," and prior demonstrations "depend on distilled checkpoints with strong prior reasoning ability, which clouds whether the method helps base models." RLP instead uses a **dense, information-theoretic reward** — the increase in log-likelihood of the actual next token when conditioning on a sampled reasoning chain versus context alone — computed verifier-free at every position, not just verifiable spans. Its own evidence is Qwen3-1.7B-Base and Nemotron-Nano-12B-v2 only: +19% average on an 8-benchmark math/science suite and 42.81%→61.32% overall respectively. A March 2026 variant, [LoopRPT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19714), applies the idea to looped/recurrent-depth architectures — further signaling an active research family, not settled production practice.

Both RPT and RLP top out at ≤12B parameters, on math/science reasoning benchmarks, using an RL-style loop layered on top of standard pretraining. Neither has disclosed evidence at 100B+ active-parameter, multi-trillion-token frontier scale. RLP's own paper is the closest thing to independent commentary on RPT available, and its critique — that RPT's positive results may be an artifact of starting from already-strong distilled checkpoints rather than a base-model-general effect — is the load-bearing caveat for anyone tempted to treat RPT as validated. The most useful outside data point, though, is Microsoft's own disclosed practice: [MAI-Thinking-1](https://microsoft.ai) (Jun 2026) describes RL as a **separate, later** hill-climbing phase layered on top of a 30T-token, human-data-only, 8,000-GPU base pretraining run ("MAI-Base-1"), with active filtering to strip AI-generated text from the crawl. This is architecturally distinct from the RPT/RLP proposal of fusing reward signal into the next-token loss during pretraining itself — a genuinely frontier-scale lab, as of mid-2026, keeps RL and pretraining as cleanly separate compute pools rather than fusing them. Treat RPT/RLP as an interesting 2026 research direction to watch, not a line item to budget into a 10T/200B curriculum.

## MTP Head Timing: A Non-Question

Worth stating because it forecloses a plausible-sounding design question: DeepSeek-V3's multi-token-prediction module is trained from scratch as an integral part of the entire 14.8T-token pretraining objective, co-optimized with the main next-token loss for the whole run, not bolted on partway through ([DeepSeek-V3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.19437)). No public source describes any lab adding an MTP head mid-run rather than from initialization — "when to add the MTP head" is not a decision point that exists in disclosed practice. Ch7 covers the MTP design choice itself (loss weight, head depth, EAGLE-style vs. DeepSeek-style); the timing question, at least, has a settled answer: from-scratch or not at all.

## The Staged 25-30T-Token Curriculum Strawman

Synthesizing the above into a concrete proposal for the 10T-total/200B-active MoE. Token fractions are anchored to disclosed practice — OLMo's 90-95%/5-10% pretrain/midtrain split, the Nemotron phase-grows-with-model-size pattern, OLMo 3's 34/66 LC/SC ratio — but every percentage below should be read as a starting hypothesis for the run's own microanneals to correct, not a settled answer; the mixture-transfer ceiling documented above means nothing here is validated at this parameter scale.

**Total budget: 28T tokens**, across four stages plus a continuous decontamination track.

| Stage | Tokens | Share | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Pretraining | ~25T | ~89% | broad-distribution base training |
| 2 — Midtraining | 2.0-2.5T | 7-9% | quality/domain upweighting |
| 3 — Long-context extension | 500-800B | 2-3% | 512K-1M context target |
| 4 — Final anneal/souping | 100-300B | 0.5-1% | variance reduction, release candidate |

**Stage 1 — Pretraining (~25T tokens).** Composition: ~65-70% filtered web (quality-classifier upsampling in the OLMo 3 style, top-5% resampled up to ~7x, bottom-40% discarded), ~12-15% code (FIM-transformed), ~8-10% math/STEM web, ~5-8% multilingual, ~2-4% academic/reference. LR schedule: WSD rather than pre-committed cosine, since a 25-30T-token run is likely to have its token budget revised mid-flight. Stability guardrails from step 0 (Ch9 owns the mechanics): repeated-n-gram filtering, reordered RMSNorm, QK-norm, z-loss, fixed RoPE θ from initialization to avoid a discontinuous scaling event later. Measurement: gradient-norm spike frequency, ≥500-domain Paloma-style held-out perplexity monthly, ranked-classification ladder eval weekly, continuous MFU/tokens-per-second. Abort/rebalance: 3-sigma gradient-norm excursions without recovery; MFU sustained >10% below target for 48h (infra issue, investigate before touching the mixture); single-domain Paloma perplexity diverging for 2+ consecutive evals (pipeline regression, not a mixture problem).

**Stage 2 — Midtraining (2.0-2.5T tokens).** This is the largest proportional departure from disclosed practice in the strawman: neither OLMo 3's 1.7% nor Nemotron 3 Super's 20% maps cleanly onto 200B active params, but the Nemotron family's model-size gradient argues for erring toward Super/Ultra's share rather than Nano's 6%. Sub-composition (of the 2.0-2.5T budget): ~35-40% quality-filtered web replay, ~15% math (synthetic plus NL-rewritten, per the +40-GSM*-point OLMo 2 finding), ~12% code, ~10% QA/knowledge-access synthetic, ~15% reasoning/thinking traces, ~8-10% instruction data **without chat special tokens**, ~3-5% PDFs/academic. Methodology: microanneals (5B+5B, dozens run in parallel) feeding integration tests (200-500B-token anneals, 4-5 rounds minimum), with roughly 1-2% of total compute (~700B-1T tokens' worth) budgeted as a distinct search line item, not absorbed silently into "midtraining." Measurement: per-round integration-test aggregate scores on OLMo-3-style categories (not saturated legacy benchmarks — see Ch18/Ch23 for the benchmark-saturation problem), domain-tradeoff checks at each round (>5-8 points of GenQA cost per math/code gain signals over-rotation), quick-SFT probes on 2-3 candidates per round, decontamination on every new source before it enters an integration test. Abort/rebalance: single-domain tradeoff worse than OLMo 3's observed range; quick-SFT gains not surviving post-training (investigate contamination-driven inflation before proceeding).

**Stage 3 — Long-context extension (500-800B tokens).** Given a 512K-1M context ambition, budget toward the Llama-3.1/DeepSeek-V3.1 end (800-840B) rather than OLMo 3/SmolLM3's ~50-100B, since no sub-200B-token LC budget has been validated at this target length. Mix: 34% long-context / 66% short-context as the default starting ratio, per OLMo 3's directly-quantified ablation, replicated at 10B-token scale before committing at full budget. Sourcing: genuinely long documents (scientific PDFs, books, repo-level code with real cross-file dependencies), not artificial concatenation; synthetic aggregation-task augmentation (extract salient noun-phrases, generate QA/summarization tasks over snippets) is worth replicating given it improved RULER scores in OLMo 3's own ablation. Technique stack: RoPE-θ scaling or YaRN on full-attention layers only, best-fit packing, intra-document masking, staged growth in 2-4 sub-stages (4K→32K→128K→512K+) rather than one jump. CP cost: plan for 32-64-way context parallelism (Nemotron 3's disclosed CP64/CP32 at a 1M target; 128-way only as contingency) and a distinct, lower MFU target for this stage specifically — noting Ch18's two placement findings: at EP64 the CP ranks necessarily land in *different* NVL72 domains, so the Ulysses all-to-all rides the slow fabric, and CP composed with trainable-sparse attention has no public reference implementation at all (a Ch24 build item, not a configuration choice). Measurement: RULER at every context bucket at each sub-stage boundary; Math/Code regression relative to end-of-Stage-2 (the single most important metric, given OLMo 3's 5-8 point documented regression pattern); tokens/s/GPU and MFU during this stage specifically to catch CP-configuration regressions. Abort/rebalance: Math/Code regression exceeding ~8-10 points (worse than OLMo 3's own worst-case 8.3 points at 32B) — increase short-context replay and re-run; MFU below ~50% of Stage-1 MFU — treat as infra escalation before burning further tokens.

**Stage 4 — Final anneal/souping (100-300B tokens).** Run 3-4 independent anneal passes from the same Stage-2 (or Stage-3, if souping post-LC) checkpoint, differing only in shuffle order, each on a 100-300B-token slice of the finalized midtrain mix with linear decay to zero. Weight-average all resulting checkpoints, budgeted explicitly as a 3-4x multiplier on anneal cost — not optional overhead, one of the best-evidenced, cheapest wins in this entire literature. Evaluate the souped checkpoint on the full eval suite before committing to it as the release candidate: do not assume every metric improves (OLMo 2's own 17.5-point GSM8k regression on one mix, even as aggregate metrics improved, is the standing counterexample). For cheaper mid-run monitoring throughout Stages 1-3, use the 4-checkpoints/1,000-steps-apart averaging trick rather than full anneal-to-zero reruns.

**Cross-cutting: decontamination.** Build or adapt `decon`'s two-phase sparse-detection/dense-cluster-expansion design, run it against every candidate source before it enters an integration test, decontaminate all splits (not just test) of the eval suite, and treat leakage removal as a hygiene requirement independent of measured score impact — per OLMo 3's own finding, removing detected contamination does not reliably move scores.

## Implications for the 10T/200B Run

Three numbers from this chapter should directly shape the run plan. First, midtraining's per-token leverage (OLMo 3's +36.3-point Math jump from 1.7% of total tokens) means the 2.0-2.5T-token Stage 2 budget above is not a rounding error against the 25T-token Stage 1 — it is plausibly the highest-ROI compute in the entire curriculum, and should be resourced, staffed, and measured accordingly rather than treated as pretraining's tail. Second, the long-context stage's documented 5-8 point Math/Code regression (worst case 8.3 points at 32B) means Stage 3 needs its own abort criteria distinct from Stage 1/2's — a run that silently absorbs an LC-stage capability regression without a dedicated gate will ship a worse model than its own Stage-2 checkpoint. Third, and most consequentially: no mixture-optimization technique in the literature (RegMix, CLIMB, Data Mixing Laws, Apple's transfer study) has been validated above roughly 1-8B target parameters, a gap of two-plus orders of magnitude from a 200B-active MoE. Every percentage in the strawman above is a hypothesis, not a plan — the run's own microanneal-plus-integration-test loop, scaled up from OLMo's proven methodology, is the actual mixture-selection mechanism, and its compute budget (Ch8's ladder-cost discipline applies directly here) needs to be sized as seriously as the pretraining FLOPs it is meant to steer.
