# Chapter 6 — The Attention Zoo: Softmax, Selection, and the Delta Rule

Attention is the block the 2026 frontier has fractured furthest, and the fracture lines are hidden behind an acronym wall: MLA, NSA, MoBA, DSA, CSA, HCA, MSA, GDN, KDA, SSD. This chapter teaches the zoo as three families with one enemy. The enemy is the **KV cache**. The families are: keep softmax and compress what it reads (latent compression); keep softmax and *select* what it reads (trainable sparsity); or delete softmax and keep a fixed-size recurrent state instead (linear attention). Every acronym above is one of these three moves plus engineering. Ch7 argues which family a 10T/200B host should bet on; this chapter is the mechanism layer, and by its end each family's characteristic failure mode — and the telemetry that would catch it — should be obvious rather than folkloric.

## The Enemy: KV-Cache Arithmetic

Softmax attention writes nothing down during training that it can reuse at inference, so serving replays it: to generate token t, each layer needs the keys and values of all t−1 predecessors, cached in HBM. The bill is per-token, per-layer: 2 × n_kv_heads × d_head × bytes. For a Llama-3.1-405B-shaped MHA/GQA model that is ~516 KB per token; a 128K-token agent session costs ~66 GB *per sequence* — before weights. Worse, decode arithmetic is **GEMV-shaped**: one query vector against t cached keys is a matrix-*vector* product, arithmetic intensity ~O(1) FLOPs per byte, pinned to the memory-bandwidth roof of the roofline (Ch16), not the FLOPs roof. DeepSeek's hardware-reflections paper states the consequence plainly: decode "shifts from GEMM to GEMV, which has a much lower compute-to-memory ratio" — the GPU idles while HBM streams cache ([arXiv:2505.09343](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.09343), May 2025). Prefill and training, by contrast, are compute-bound GEMMs. So every attention design serves two masters with opposite preferences — train/prefill wants maximum expressivity per FLOP, decode wants minimum bytes per token — and the three families are three theories of how to satisfy both. Quadratic *training* cost (attention FLOPs ∝ L² per layer) is the secondary enemy, decisive only at very long context; at a 4-8K pretraining sequence length, attention is 30-40% of FLOPs (Ch7) and the cache, not the square, is what hurts.

One 2017 pathology needs naming before the families make sense: softmax must allocate exactly probability 1 across the context even when a head has nothing to say. Heads dump that forced allocation onto semantically empty positions — **attention sinks** (usually token 0), the StreamingLLM finding. The 2026 fix is honest bookkeeping: a learnable per-head sink logit in the softmax denominator (gpt-oss, DeepSeek-V4), letting heads attend to *nothing* explicitly. It costs one scalar per head and removes a whole class of first-token artifacts; there is little reason for a new design to omit it.

## Family Zero: Shrinking Softmax Honestly — MQA, GQA, MLA

**MQA/GQA** attack cache bytes at the source: share one K/V head across all (MQA) or groups of (GQA) query heads — n_kv_heads falls from 128 to 8 (Llama-3-70B) or 1, cache shrinks proportionally, quality dips mildly. Every frontier model without latent compression ships GQA; it is the boring baseline the rest of this chapter is trying to beat.

**MLA (Multi-head Latent Attention)** — DeepSeek's V2-era mechanism, now the single most consequential attention design of the MoE era — is best taught as a factorization trick with one obstruction. Instead of caching per-head K and V, cache one shared low-rank **latent** c_t = W_down·h_t (d_c=512 in V3, against d_model=7168); every head's K and V are up-projections of c. The magic is **absorption**: at decode, q·k = qᵀW_upᵀc can be regrouped as (W_upᵀq)ᵀ·c — fold the key up-projection into the query projection (and the value up-projection into the output projection) and *the per-head K/V never exist*. All 128 heads attend directly over the same 512-dim latent, as if the model were an MQA with a 512-dim shared head. Cache: 512 + 64 = 576 dims/layer ≈ **70 KB/token** for V3 — 7.3× under the 405B MHA figure, 2.7-4.7× under comparable GQA. The +64 is the obstruction: RoPE's position-dependent rotation sits *between* W_up and the dot product, so it can't be absorbed. DeepSeek's solution is a small **decoupled RoPE branch** — 64 rotated dims carried alongside the 512 position-free latent dims — which is why Partial RoPE (Ch5) is load-bearing here, not an aesthetic.

Why does compressing 128 heads into a 512-dim bottleneck not *hurt*? Su Jianlin's two-part analysis — the sharpest public theory on this — reframes MLA as holding two identities at once: during training/prefill (compute-bound) it behaves like an MHA with generous ~192 effective head dims; during decode (bandwidth-bound) it *is* a 576-dim shared-KV MQA, via absorption, with the same weights. Nothing is "traded": decode is never compute-bound, so the extra decode-side FLOPs the absorbed form implies are free. His ablations decompose MLA's win into ranked parts — bigger effective head_dims (the dominant lever, bigger than more heads or wider FFN at matched params), Partial RoPE (independently positive), KV-sharing (smallest) — and his GQA2-(192+64) variant with VO-RoPE lands within 0.003 nats of MLA at the same cache budget ([kexue.fm #20](https://kexue.fm/archives/10907), [#21](https://kexue.fm/archives/11111), May/Jul 2025). The design freedom that actually matters, per this decomposition: push head_dims past the conventional 128 and choose your RoPE fraction — not "copy MLA verbatim or don't." That finding drives Ch7's attention row.

## Family One: Trainable Sparse Selection

The selection family's principle: keep softmax attention *exact* over k entries, and spend a small learned mechanism deciding *which* k — collapsing cost from O(L²) toward O(L·k) while retaining softmax's arbitrary-precision retrieval over whatever it does read. Fixed-pattern ancestors (sliding windows; Gemma 3's 5:1 local:global ratio; gpt-oss's alternating layers; Llama 4's iRoPE) hard-code the selection; the 2025-2026 generation *learns* it, and learning it turns out to be cheap.

**NSA** (DeepSeek+PKU, ACL 2025 Best Paper) established the template: three parallel branches per head — compressed summaries (coarse), top-k selected blocks (fine), and a sliding window (recent) — merged by learned gates, trained natively from step one so the selection behavior is shaped by pretraining rather than retrofitted ([arXiv:2502.11089](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.11089), Feb 2025). **MoBA** (Moonshot) restates selection as MoE logic applied to attention — queries route to KV *blocks* via top-k over block-mean keys — and serves Kimi's production long-context traffic ([arXiv:2502.13189](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.13189), Feb 2025, paper + code). **DSA** (V3.2-Exp) is the fully-specified production recipe: a tiny FP8 **lightning indexer** (ReLU-scored, few-headed, cheap on purpose) scores all history per query; main MLA-under-MQA attention runs exactly on the top-k=2048 entries. It is *retrofitted* onto a dense checkpoint — a 2.1B-token warm-up trains the indexer alone via KL against the dense model's own attention distribution (the model teaches its own index where it looks), then ~944B sparse-training tokens adapt everything — and the measured bill at 128K is ~5× cheaper decode with benchmark parity (V3.2-Exp report, Sep 2025). GLM-5 adopting DSA *by name* made it the first cross-lab-standardized sparse mechanism ([GLM-5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15763), 2026).

The June 2026 developments radicalize both ends. **V4's CSA+HCA** move selection *onto compressed memory*: every m tokens are summarized into one KV entry (softmax-weighted pooling over overlapping 2m windows); CSA layers run indexer-selected sparse attention over those summaries, HCA layers compress far harder (m′≫m) and attend densely over the tiny result; layers interleave the two, each keeping an uncompressed sliding window for the recent past. Attention becomes explicitly hierarchical memory — verbatim recent, addressable summarized mid-range, gist-only far — and the bill at 1M context is 27% of V3.2's per-token FLOPs and 10% of its cache for V4-Pro ([arXiv:2606.19348](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19348), 2026). **MiniMax's MSA** shows the selection principle is backbone-agnostic: a two-branch design (index branch picks KV blocks, main branch runs exact softmax over them) on a plain GQA backbone — no latent compression anywhere — reporting GQA-parity quality with 28.4× less per-token attention compute at 1M, validated at 109B/3T tokens and shipped in M3, MiniMax's 1M-context production flagship ([arXiv:2606.13392](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13392), Jun 2026; [M3 launch](https://www.minimax.io/blog/minimax-m3)). MSA completes an arc Ch3 tells in full: MiniMax's trajectory is linear (M1) → full (M2) → *sparse-softmax* (M3) — the lab that published the field's only quantified linear-attention retreat re-attacked the same cost problem one year later from the selection side. That is the strongest single datapoint for the claim that **selection, not state-compression, is where the risk-averse end of the frontier is converging**: it preserves softmax's exact-retrieval semantics (the very thing whose loss burned M1) while buying the same asymptotic relief.

Selection's characteristic failure mode, for the telemetry-minded: the indexer is a second, smaller model whose errors are silent — a miss simply excludes evidence from attention. Indexer-vs-dense KL (DSA's own training signal) and long-range retrieval evals (RULER-class, multi-hop) are the canaries, and the retrofit path's cheapness (~1T tokens on a dense base) is a genuine de-risking property Ch7 leans on: you can *decide late*.

## Family Two: Linear Attention, Actually Explained

Delete softmax and attention becomes algebra you can reorder. Unnormalized attention output at position t is Σ_{s≤t}(q_t·k_s)v_s; without the softmax coupling q to every k, this regroups as q_t·(Σ_{s≤t} k_sᵀv_s) — and that sum is a single **d_k×d_v matrix S_t, updatable in O(1) per token**: S_t = S_{t−1} + k_tᵀv_t, output q_t·S_t. The cache is gone; in its place a *fixed-size* state, constant in sequence length. Everything else in this family is commentary on one question: **what should a fixed-size matrix remember?**

Read S as an **associative memory**: k_tᵀv_t is a rank-1 write binding key k_t to value v_t; q_t·S_t is content-addressed read-out; capacity is d_k×d_v numbers regardless of context length. The naive update above is Hebbian accumulation, and it fails in two instructive ways: writes to correlated keys *interfere* (retrieval returns a superposition of every value ever bound near k), and the state grows without bound. The family's evolution is successive repairs to the write rule:

- **Decay** (RetNet's fixed exponential; Mamba-2 and GLA's *input-dependent* gates): S_t = α_t·S_{t−1} + k_tᵀv_t, α_t∈(0,1) learned per step (scalar per head in Mamba-2, per-channel in GLA). Forgetting bounds the state and prioritizes recency — and this is also where the "SSM versus linear attention" distinction dissolves: Mamba-2's state-space duality result shows selective SSMs *are* decay-gated linear attention in different notation ([arXiv:2405.21060](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.21060), May 2024). One family, two literatures. Nemotron-3's "Mamba-Transformer hybrid" and Qwen3-Next's "Gated DeltaNet hybrid" are siblings, not rivals.
- **The delta rule** (DeltaNet): decay forgets *indiscriminately* — old associations fade whether or not they're stale. The delta rule instead does targeted replacement: before writing (k_t, v_new), *read* the state's current belief v_old = S_{t−1}·k_t and write the difference: S_t = S_{t−1}(I − β_t k_tᵀk_t) + β_t k_tᵀv_t — erase what k_t currently retrieves, then bind the new value, with write-strength β_t. This is exactly one step of online gradient descent on the memory's retrieval error ‖S·k_t − v_t‖², which is why the 2025-2026 literature reframes recurrence as **test-time learning**: the state is a tiny fast-weight model being trained, token by token, inside the forward pass. Interference — the Hebbian killer — is addressed rather than merely decayed away. The 2024 parallelization breakthrough (chunkwise WY-representation: process the sequence in chunks, materializing each chunk's compound rank-k update as two skinny matrices, quadratic-form *inside* chunks, recurrent *across* them) is what made delta-rule models trainable at tensor-core speeds at all — the same intra/inter-chunk split that "lightning attention" (TransNormer lineage) applies to plain decay-gated linear attention, and the reason MiniMax could train M1's 456B hybrid without drowning in a sequential scan.
- **Decay + delta, then finer gates**: **Gated DeltaNet** composes both repairs — S_t = α_t·S_{t−1}(I − β_t k_tᵀk_t) + β_t k_tᵀv_t (forget a little globally, overwrite precisely where writing) (NVIDIA; [arXiv:2412.06464](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.06464), Dec 2024); Qwen3-Next ships it 3:1 against gated full attention. **KDA (Kimi Delta Attention)** refines GDN with *per-channel* decay (a Diag(a_t) forget-gate — different memory channels age at different rates) and, critically, a hardware-shaped chunkwise algorithm: KDA's transition has Diagonal-Plus-Low-Rank structure but with the low-rank factors *tied* to the decay vector, roughly halving the matmuls of the general DPLR form — the difference between an elegant recurrence and one that sustains ~100% better operator throughput and 6× decode at 1M in a production 48B ([Kimi Linear](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26692), Oct 2025, paper + code). **RWKV-7** carries the same generalized-delta machinery to the no-full-attention-at-all extreme at sub-3B scale — the family's pure-recurrence boundary marker, useful as an existence proof and as a warning that nobody has shipped it alone at frontier scale.

Why does every *frontier* deployment hybridize (M1 at 7:1, Kimi Linear/K3 and Qwen3-Next at 3:1, Nemotron-3 with sparse "global anchors")? Because the fixed-size state is a **lossy summary by construction** — exact needle retrieval over arbitrary distance, induction-head copying, and multi-hop composition provably need either unbounded memory or re-reading, and the interleaved full-attention layers supply exactly that (their KV cache, amortized over the ratio, is the design's real cache bill — hence "75% reduction," not 100%). The hybrid ratio is a dial between softmax's exactness and the state's economy. And the family's production failure modes, per MiniMax's M1→M2 postmortem (Ch3), are exactly where this mechanism analysis predicts: *precision fragility* (the state is a running sum accumulated across thousands of steps — low-precision storage compounds rounding error in a way stateless softmax, which reduces over the sequence fresh each time, structurally cannot), *memory-bound training* (chunkwise recurrences stream state through HBM; without heroic kernel IO the FLOPs roof is unreachable), and *benchmark blindness* (short-context evals cannot see a lossy long-range state — the deficits emerged at scale, on multi-hop tasks, after the architecture was frozen). K3 betting KDA at 2.8T against that postmortem — with Moonshot's kernel stack and, per Ch10's NVFP4-RL addendum lineage, explicit per-token-scale precision contracts — is the live experiment on whether those are maturity problems or physics.

## The Map, and What to Watch

The three families in one table — mechanism, cache scaling, exactness, dominant risk, flagship carrier:

| Family | Mechanism | Cache/state at length L | Retrieval | Dominant risk | 2026 carriers |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Latent compression | low-rank shared KV + absorption | O(L), ~7× smaller constant | exact | none new — the conservative move | DeepSeek V3→V4, GLM-4.5, K2/K2.5 |
| Trainable selection | learned index → exact softmax on top-k | O(L) stored, O(k) read | exact over selected; silent misses | indexer quality (KL drift, retrieval evals) | DSA→GLM-5, V4 CSA/HCA, MiniMax M3 (MSA), MoBA |
| Linear/recurrent (+hybrid) | fixed-size fast-weight state, delta/decay writes | O(1) per linear layer + full-attn share | lossy; hybrids restore | precision of state, memory-bound kernels, eval blindness | Kimi Linear→K3 (KDA), Qwen3-Next (GDN), Nemotron-3 (Mamba-2), RWKV-7 (pure) |

Note what the map implies about compositionality: the families stack. V4 runs selection *and* compression (CSA's indexer over compressed entries, under MLA-style MQA); K3 runs linear *and* latent (KDA layers between Gated-MLA globals); nothing prevents a design from drawing one mechanism per regime — recurrent local, selected mid-range, compressed far — and V4's CSA/HCA interleave is recognizably halfway there. The acronyms will keep coming; the moves won't. Every attention headline from here on should parse as: *which family, which write rule or index, which ratio, what's the precision contract on the state or the miss-telemetry on the index.*

## Implications for the 10T/200B Run

First, **cache arithmetic before ideology**: the strawman's serving economics (Ch23) are set by bytes/token/layer, and this chapter's numbers — 516 KB (MHA), ~190-330 KB (GQA), 70 KB (MLA), ~70 KB × (1+ratio)⁻¹-ish for hybrids, O(k) reads for selection — are the actual decision variables; run them against the target context distribution before any quality argument. Second, **selection is the de-riskable bet**: DSA's retrofit recipe means a 10T/200B program can pretrain dense-attention (or MLA), then convert for ~1T tokens *after* the capability profile is measurable — no other family offers that reversibility, and MiniMax's full trajectory (linear→full→sparse) is one lab discovering the same asymmetry the hard way. Third, **if betting on linear-hybrid anyway** (the K3 path — real, and by K3's existence no longer dismissible), the mechanism analysis dictates the contract: per-channel-gated delta-rule variants with tied-DPLR-class kernels or better, an explicit precision spec for state accumulation (BF16 floors, defined rounding — Ch10's discipline applied to a new tensor), hybrid ratio held ≥1:4 until long-context multi-hop evals at target scale say otherwise, and a RULER/MTOB-class eval battery *in the ablation ladder, before freeze* — because the one documented failure of this family was a failure of evaluation coverage, not of the delta rule. Fourth, **watch the state-tracking literature**: the delta rule's test-time-learning framing is spawning richer write rules (higher-order updates, momentum-like state optimizers) faster than any of them is validating at scale; the correct posture for a 2026-2027 run is the recurring one — boring core, one funded exotic ablation, telemetry that would catch the exotic failing early.
